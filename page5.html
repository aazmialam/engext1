REFLECTION

A Case of Identity offers a diverse range of potential detective fiction appropriation options. The strong element of love, detective fiction features and the underlying feature of disguise appealed to me strongly, and encouraged me to select it for appropriation. In order to appropriate, I investigated the architecture and geography of modern day London, England, the setting of the story. This allowed for the intricacies of the story to be better highlighted. The gender of the story characters remained the same, however, the state of them were changed to enhance the updated plot. An additional character, was introduced, Austin Sutherland, brother of Mary Sutherland. This allowed for the narration to be smoother and voiced appropriately. The creation of a title, was a challenging experience which involved creative thinking, and was finalised to become Deadly Dating. The appropriation consists of a range of narrative voices, which allowed for the story to be conveyed on a wider spectrum. Overall this task served to be challenging as it required the use of a creative mindset.
